So... It's been a while.

First of all, I should apolog- nah, honestly, not really. {{ dmoji(name="xdd") }}

But seriously, what happened? Why did I disappear? It's not like many people noticed, but I definitely did. And the truth is, believe it or not, every day without releasing an update makes me feel a little worse.

Let's talk about [Ambxst](/), which hasn't been updated in over a month. I've been learning Go, Rust, and Zig, trying to make the most out of this new knowledge to deliver a better experience. However, this has consumed a lot of my time and energy, resulting in a lack of regular updates. On top of that, university has also been pretty demanding, which contributed to my absence. And not only that, but I've also had to w-w-w-w-work... {{ dmoji(name="perturbated") }}

Well, I don't really have a stable job at all. I repair computers, nothing much beyond that. It usually takes me one or two days per client, but I learn things and make some money, so I can't really complain. {{ dmoji(name="tiredmonster") }}

Still, if you'd like to support me, you can always throw a few bucks my way on [Ko-fi](https://ko-fi.com/Axenide). {{ dmoji(name="oldsmiley") }}

(I'm planning to make my own donation page soon, but for now we're sticking with Ko-fi.)

Also, unfortunately, I have to move. I hate moving, but it's for the good of my family. We'll be able to save more money and stuff, but... Ugh, I don't really want to go into too much detail, but honestly this change stresses me out a lot. I don't want to leave.

It's not far away, so it's not like I'm going to lose contact with my friends or anything, but... I don't want to leave. {{ dmoji(name="Sadge") }}

Back to Ambxst, my attempt to port all of its logic to Go turned into a long and complicated process, and unfortunately... It remained mostly a learning experience. In other words, I failed. At least for now.

I managed to port quite a few things, but the experience became significantly worse. Performance turned into a disaster, and the user experience became unbearable.

And it's absolutely not Go's fault, it's mine. I genuinely LOVE Go, it's an incredibly powerful and efficient language, but between Ambxst being a pretty complex project and me being a beginner in Go, the result was catastrophic. {{ dmoji(name="Sadge") }}

So I reverted everything. I went back to the previous version, and while it's not as efficient as I'd like, at least it's functional. And that's what matters for now. Plus, there are many PRs that can finally be merged now, which wouldn't have happened if I had continued with the Go version. {{ dmoji(name="skl") }}

When I get another chance, I'll try again.

Still, I've had some peace of mind because Ambxst is currently *relatively* stable. I use it every single day, which forces me to fix things whenever something critical shows up.

Ah... I miss those days where I only had a Polybar/Waybar and Rofi setup... But there were limits to overcome.

Well... What else? Oh, right. Other things I've been working on:

* An AI assistant:
  Thanks to the rise of OpenClaw and similar projects, I felt obligated to finally get serious about my own version — something I've dreamed about since... 2017?
  Those familiar with my *lore* know that for a very long time I've wanted to create my own AI companion, and now that the technology has advanced enough, I can finally make it real.
  It's not something I'm planning to release anytime soon, but I'm really excited about its potential.
  This actually resembles the second half of the story for the game I want to develop, BUT I MUST NOT TALK ABOUT THAT.

* A new project (maybe):
  This also remains mostly a learning project, at least for now, but I've been thinking about a possible successor to Ambxst.
  Ever since I got into Linux ricing, I've always been fascinated by AwesomeWM setups. I love their flexibility and customization.
  The problem is that it's X11, and Wayland is the future.
  BUT...! I found SomeWM. It's basically a PORT of AWESOMEWM.
  I love it. Many configurations already work without changing anything, and it's incredibly fast.
  However, it's still a pretty young project and there's a lot left to improve.
  But if I could provide the Ambxst experience as a configuration layer for SomeWM, it could become something much better in certain aspects. Compatibility, performance, stability, dependencies... Since it'd practically just be SomeWM and maybe a couple Lua libraries.
  And while many people are asking me for MangoWM, Niri, and other compositor support (which is actually pretty close), considering SomeWM's flexibility, we'd basically have every layout imaginable, any kind of workflow, whatever we want.
  But well, it's still just a local experiment. I don't even fully understand SomeWM's limits yet, so I can't promise anything.

* Reviving my YouTube channel:
  I really miss making videos. It's something that still itches in the back of my mind — it never stopped.
  At one point I think I lost myself trying to chase popularity (unsuccessfully), but through that I realized that what I truly wanted was something I already had:
  I just wanted to create content.

  The thing is, I want to come back big, fulfilling a fantasy I've had for years: making a web series.

  Well, technically it'd still be the same kind of videos I've always made — obviously talking about topics I love, and nowadays probably much more focused on technology and science — but with a stronger narrative, characters, and so on.
  It's not something coming anytime soon, but it's something I really want to do.

  I feel like it's a way to *bypass* the algorithm (which is technically a neural network, but we'll talk about that another time) by creating some sort of ongoing narrative that keeps people interested without trapping myself into always talking about the same thing.

  That's actually why, over a year ago, I tried learning 3D.
  And honestly, I did pretty well considering I only spent a few hours with Blender, but I still have a LOT to learn before I can make something decent.

  And as you probably know, if something isn't decent, I don't release it. {{ dmoji(name="angryasf") }}

Anyway, that's all for now.
I didn't want to make such a long update, but I had a lot to say.
I hope you're all doing well, and thanks for sticking around despite my absence. {{ dmoji(name="sans") }}
